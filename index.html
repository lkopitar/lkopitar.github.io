Who I am

Researcher and teaching assistant

Slovenian, Koper-based bioinformatician, who has been always interested in the interdisciplinarity of biology-related topics and computer science, eventually became a data scientist. Alike other data scientists, most of my time is spent in data wrangling, utilization of machine learning algorithms and interpretability of models.
When not dealing with data, I am most likely to be found on the athletics stadium, in the gym or somewhere in the nature, regaining my energy by discovering new places, locally and abroad.

My interests

Machine learning (AI)

With the rise of the use of technology, the presence of machine learning emerged all around us. Curiousity about how machine learning models operate and make decisions instead of us, in just a few miliseconds, has always been fascinating. Furthermore, the idea of writing several lines of code, which then brings up a result, even if it goes for a convoluted graphical image, makes you feel proud and satisfaction.

Health & healthy lifestyle

The borderline of healthy and unhealthy lifestyle has never been that marginal. The traps and causes can be hidden in various products, long workhours, sedentary/unhealthy lifestyle and in many other ways. Aware of that, my life has been always oriented towards that problematic, beginning with actively participating in physical activities from the early age, balancing my eating habits and proceeding with conducting research in healthcare related issues.

Application of ML in healthcare/medicine

Combining previous interests can lead to novel discoveries in health-care area, and therefore improving the quality of life of people worldwide. Whether the contribution is provided indirectly (published article, manuscripts) or directly (deploying a Web Application), the feeling that your contribution is valuable can be undescribable.
