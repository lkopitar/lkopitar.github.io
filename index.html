Who I am

Researcher and teaching assistant

Slovenian, Koper-based bioinformatician, who has been always interested in the interdisciplinarity of biology-related topics and computer science, eventually became a data scientist. Alike other data scientists, most of my time is spent in data wrangling, utilization of machine learning algorithms, and interpretability of models.
When not dealing with data, I am most likely to be found on the athletics stadium, in the gym or somewhere in the nature, regaining my energy by discovering new places, locally and abroad.

My interests

Machine learning (AI)

With the rise of technology, the prevalence of machine learning has emerged all around us. The curiosity about how machine learning models operate and make decisions in just a few milliseconds has always been fascinating. Furthermore, the idea of writing several lines of code that can produce results, even if it involves processing convoluted graphical images, instills a sense of pride and satisfaction.

Health & healthy lifestyle

The boundary between a healthy and unhealthy lifestyle has never been so subtle. Traps and causes can lurk in various products, prolonged work hours, sedentary routines, and other aspects of daily life. Mindful of this, I have always oriented my life towards addressing these challenges. It began with actively participating in physical activities from an early age, and I have consistently maintained a balanced approach to my eating habits. Today, my passion for promoting well-being continues as I engage in research on healthcare and medicine-related issues.

Application of ML in healthcare/medicine

Combining previous interests can lead to novel discoveries in the healthcare/medical field, thereby enhancing the quality of life for people worldwide. Whether the contribution is made indirectly through published articles or directly through deploying a Web Application, the feeling that your input is valuable can be indescribable.
